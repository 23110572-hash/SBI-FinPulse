About the Hackathon
As part of Global Fintech Fest (GFF) 2026, State Bank of India is launching a hackathon focused on identifying innovative solutions for customer acquisition, digital adoption, and digital engagement within the banking ecosystem.

The hackathon aims to bring together fintechs and startups to develop technology-driven solutions that can help improve banking services, accelerate digital transformation, and create meaningful customer experiences at scale.

Participants will be required to develop working prototypes addressing SBI's identified problem statements under the AI & Emerging Tech track.

Problem Statement
Use of Agentic AI for Customer Acquisition, Digital Adoption & Digital Engagement
Banks today face increasing challenges in acquiring customers at scale, driving adoption of digital products, and creating meaningful long-term engagement.

This hackathon invites startups and fintechs to build next-generation Agentic AI solutions that can autonomously assist banks in:

Customer acquisition and onboarding

Digital product adoption

Personalised customer engagement

Intelligent financial interactions

AI-led banking journeys

Focus Areas

Pillar 01
Customer Acquisition
Develop solutions that can intelligently identify, qualify, and convert customers through hyper-personalised engagement and conversational onboarding.

Pillar 02
Digital Adoption
Build contextual and adaptive AI experiences that encourage adoption of digital banking services including payments, investments, insurance, and mobile banking.

Pillar 03
Digital Engagement
Create AI-driven engagement models that proactively interact with customers based on behaviours, financial patterns, and life events.

Evaluation Criteria - Important
Prototypes will be evaluated by SBI's jury panel across six dimensions:

Innovation
Originality and creativity in solving the problem statement.
Technical Feasibility
Practicality and feasibility of the prototype in real-world banking applications.
Business Potential
Commercial viability and scalability of the proposed solution.
Scalability
Ability to scale the solution across large customer segments and use cases.
User Experience
Ease of use, accessibility, and customer-centric design.
Regulatory Readiness
Alignment with RBI guidelines, DPDP Act, and applicable data privacy frameworks.
