# Insurance Guide

## Why Insurance Comes Before Investment
Insurance protects your finances from catastrophic loss. Secure protection (term + health) before chasing investment returns.

## Term Insurance
- Pure life cover — pays your family a lump sum if you pass away during the term.
- Rule of thumb: cover ≈ **10–15× your annual income**, plus outstanding loans.
- Buy early — premiums are far cheaper when you are young and healthy.
- It is the cheapest, most efficient form of life cover (no investment component).

## Health Insurance
- Covers hospitalisation costs; protects savings from medical emergencies.
- Get a family floater of at least ₹10 lakh in metros.
- Check waiting periods, room-rent limits, and network hospitals.

## What to Avoid
- Don't mix insurance and investment unknowingly (endowment/ULIP often give low returns). For pure protection, prefer term insurance + separate investments.

## Tax Benefits
- Life insurance premium: Section 80C.
- Health insurance premium: Section 80D.

## When to Review
- After major life events: marriage, a child, a home loan, a salary hike. Increase cover to match new responsibilities.
