# Tax Planning Basics (India)

## Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh deduction)
Eligible investments/expenses: PPF, ELSS mutual funds, 5-year tax-saving FD, life insurance premium, EPF, principal repayment on home loan, Sukanya Samriddhi, NSC.

## Section 80CCD(1B) (extra ₹50,000)
Additional deduction for NPS contributions, over and above the 80C limit.

## Section 80D (health insurance premium)
- Up to ₹25,000 for self/family.
- Additional ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if senior citizen) for parents.

## Section 80E
Deduction on interest paid on an education loan (no upper limit, for up to 8 years).

## Section 24(b)
Deduction up to ₹2 lakh on home loan interest for a self-occupied property.

## Old vs New Regime
- The new tax regime offers lower slab rates but removes most deductions.
- The old regime rewards those who invest in 80C/80D instruments.
- Compare both based on your deductions before choosing.

## Capital Gains
- Equity LTCG (>1 yr): 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh/year.
- Equity STCG (≤1 yr): 20%.
- Plan redemptions to use the annual exemption efficiently.
