# Investment Basics

## What is a SIP?
A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount (as low as ₹500) every month into a mutual fund. It builds discipline and averages your purchase price across market ups and downs (rupee-cost averaging).

## Power of Compounding
Compounding means your returns also earn returns. Example: ₹5,000/month for 10 years at 12% p.a. grows to about **₹11.6 lakh** on just ₹6 lakh invested. Over 20 years it becomes roughly **₹50 lakh**. Time in the market matters more than timing the market.

## FD vs MF vs PPF vs NPS
- **FD**: fixed ~6.5–7% return, very safe, fully liquid (with penalty), interest taxable.
- **Mutual Fund**: market-linked, ~10–14% long-term potential, higher risk, best for long goals.
- **PPF**: ~7.1%, 15-year lock-in, tax-free (EEE), very safe.
- **NPS**: market-linked retirement, extra ₹50k tax break under 80CCD(1B), partial lock-in till 60.

## Risk vs Return
Higher potential returns come with higher risk and volatility. Match the product to your time horizon: short-term goals → debt/FD; long-term goals → equity.

## Asset Allocation Rule of Thumb
A common guideline: equity allocation ≈ (100 − your age)%. A 30-year-old might keep ~70% in equity, a 60-year-old ~40%.

## Key Terms
- **NAV**: Net Asset Value — the per-unit price of a mutual fund.
- **Expense ratio**: annual fee charged by the fund (lower is better).
- **CAGR**: Compound Annual Growth Rate — the smoothed annual return over a period.
