# Budgeting Basics

## The 50/30/20 Rule
- **50%** of income on needs (rent, groceries, utilities, EMIs).
- **30%** on wants (dining, shopping, entertainment, travel).
- **20%** on savings and investments (SIP, emergency fund, insurance).

## Emergency Fund
Keep **3–6 months of essential expenses** in a liquid, safe place (savings account or liquid FD). This protects you from job loss or unexpected costs without breaking long-term investments.

## Track Your Spending
Categorise expenses monthly. Identify "leaks" — small recurring spends (subscriptions, frequent food delivery) that add up.

## Pay Yourself First
Automate your SIP/savings on salary day, before discretionary spending. Treat savings like a non-negotiable bill.

## Avoid Lifestyle Inflation
When income rises, increase savings before increasing spending. A salary hike is the best time to start or step up an SIP.

## Manage Debt
Keep total EMIs under ~40% of income. Prioritise paying off high-interest debt (credit cards, personal loans) first.
