# SBI Mutual Funds

SBI Mutual Fund (SBI Funds Management) is one of India's largest AMCs. Investments are subject to market risks; read all scheme related documents carefully. Past performance is not indicative of future returns.

## SBI Bluechip Fund (Large Cap)
- Invests in large, well-established companies.
- Risk: Moderate. Suitable for first-time equity investors.
- Minimum SIP: **₹500/month**; minimum lump sum ₹5,000.
- Long-term (5yr+) returns historically in the ~12–14% p.a. range (varies with market).

## SBI Small Cap Fund
- Invests in small-cap companies with high growth potential.
- Risk: Very High. Suitable for aggressive investors with 7+ year horizon.
- Higher volatility, higher potential return.

## SBI Equity Hybrid Fund
- Mix of equity (65–80%) and debt — balanced risk.
- Suitable for moderate investors who want some stability.

## SBI Focused Equity Fund
- Concentrated portfolio (limited number of high-conviction stocks).
- Risk: High. For experienced investors.

## SBI Magnum Medium Duration Fund (Debt)
- Invests in debt and money-market instruments.
- Lower risk than equity; suitable for 3–4 year goals.

## SBI Magnum Children's Benefit Fund
- Goal-based fund for children's future (education/marriage).
- Has a lock-in until the child attains majority or 5 years (whichever earlier).

## How SIP Works
- SIP = Systematic Investment Plan: invest a fixed amount monthly.
- Rupee-cost averaging smooths out market volatility.
- Example: ₹5,000/month for 10 years at 12% p.a. ≈ ₹11.6 lakh corpus on ₹6 lakh invested (power of compounding).

## Tax Implications
- Equity funds: LTCG (held > 1 year) taxed at 12.5% above ₹1.25 lakh gains/year; STCG (≤ 1 year) at 20%.
- Debt funds: gains taxed at the investor's slab rate.
- ELSS (tax-saving equity funds) qualify for 80C deduction with a 3-year lock-in.

## How to Start
- Via the **YONO app** → Investments → Mutual Funds, or sbimf.com.
- Complete one-time KYC, choose scheme, set SIP date and amount.
