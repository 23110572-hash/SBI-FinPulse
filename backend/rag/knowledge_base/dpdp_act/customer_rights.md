# DPDP Act 2023 — Data Principal Rights (Summary)

A Data Principal (the customer whose data is processed) has these rights:

- **Right to access**: Obtain a summary of personal data being processed and the processing activities.
- **Right to correction and erasure**: Correct inaccurate/incomplete data and request erasure when no longer needed.
- **Right to grievance redressal**: A readily available means to raise grievances with the Data Fiduciary; escalation to the Data Protection Board.
- **Right to nominate**: Nominate another individual to exercise rights in case of death or incapacity.
- **Right to withdraw consent**: Withdraw at any time, as easily as it was given.

## Duties of the Data Principal
- Provide authentic information; not impersonate others or file frivolous complaints.

## Obligations on the Bank (Data Fiduciary)
- Process lawfully with consent or for legitimate uses.
- Maintain accuracy, implement security safeguards, and respond to rights requests within prescribed timelines.
