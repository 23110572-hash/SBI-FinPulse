# DPDP Act 2023 — Consent Requirements (Summary)

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 governs processing of personal data in India.

## Valid Consent
Consent must be **free, specific, informed, unconditional and unambiguous**, given through a clear affirmative action. It must be as easy to withdraw as to give.

## Notice
Before or at the time of seeking consent, the Data Fiduciary must give a notice describing:
- the personal data to be collected,
- the purpose of processing,
- how to exercise rights and file complaints.

## Purpose Limitation
Personal data may be used **only for the specific purpose** for which consent was given. Using customer transaction data for marketing requires explicit marketing consent.

## Data Minimisation
Collect only the personal data **necessary** for the stated purpose.

## Right to Withdraw
The Data Principal (customer) can withdraw consent at any time; processing must stop (subject to legal retention requirements).

## Consent Manager
Customers may give, manage, review and withdraw consent through a registered Consent Manager.

## Relevance to Nudges
Behavioural nudges that use transaction data require valid marketing consent obtained at onboarding, and the customer must be able to opt out.
