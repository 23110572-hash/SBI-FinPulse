# RBI Fair Practices Code (Summary)

The RBI Fair Practices Code governs how banks and lenders deal with customers. Key principles relevant to product recommendations:

- **No misselling**: Products must be sold based on the customer's actual needs and suitability, not on incentives. Do not push unsuitable products.
- **Suitability assessment**: Assess the customer's financial situation, risk appetite and objectives before recommending investment or insurance products.
- **Transparency**: All terms, charges, interest rates and risks must be disclosed clearly and upfront. No hidden charges.
- **Informed consent**: The customer must understand what they are buying. Provide a Key Fact Statement for loans.
- **Right to refuse**: Customers have the right to decline any product without pressure. Respect a "no".
- **Non-discriminatory**: Do not discriminate on the basis of gender, religion, caste, etc.
- **Grievance redressal**: A clear complaint mechanism and Banking Ombudsman recourse must be available.
- **Privacy**: Customer information must be kept confidential and used only for stated purposes.

For investment/insurance nudges: always state that returns are not guaranteed, that mutual funds carry market risk, and that insurance is subject to solicitation.
